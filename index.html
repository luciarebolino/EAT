_WRITING _01. Listen to the World by The New York Times _01.1 LANDSCAPE OF ENERGY

The experience of sound as an invisible form of interference. It is generated in the void where the atmosphere moves imperceptibly, and the space takes on a sonorous dimension that is intensified when the noises and sounds of ordinary landscapes are decoded.

The senses that perceive space, such as sight and touch, are distorted by the perception of the void, constantly changing in time, compressed and stretched by the forces surrounding it, producing sounds. In the scientific imaginary, according to the principles of sound engineering, vibration naturally spreads through space in an oscillatory manner, sound waves. The concept of interference, the disturbance of the enviroment that allow us to perceive the sound, is highilighted in the relationship between man and nature and it is visible in the landscapes of Northern Italy, where the cable of the Teleferica is a static and immobile element, human made, settled in the rural/mountainous landscape, read as the infrastructure of an industrial archaeology.

The landscape of energy is a fascinating subject. Energy moving through bodies and voids modifies the shapes of the space we perceive, but without tools and technology, our bodies cannot perceive this super-space. The sound experience allowed me to discover and dwell on the dimension of movement that exists in such 'primitive' structures of Italian energy landscapes. Sound takes on a force, a displacement, it becomes a vector. A historical monument, a tensile structure in which the cables of the Teleferica are only the structural element, and the sound, futuristic, becomes the technology with which we read the contemporary landscape.

The idea that the sound moves directionally according to the structure of the cables generates a visual imagery of this space in which propagation covers the void that exists between two extremes/valleys directionally. Vectors of intensity move in the void, as an extension of the forest that speaks through the cables, the ends of branches that extended carrying matter and non-matter.

The cables resonate with the action of external agents, wind, temperature, rain and air pressure. Oscillation and intervals turn off and on as insects, birds and branches touching the zip lines create intense tonal reverberations. How can we visualise energetic space? Designing non-matter to create an experience that makes the invisibility of energy infrastructure perceptible is an experimental topic that can be investigated using contemporary tools and technologies to enable physical visualisation in space.
